1. Digital Marketplace overview

The Digital Marketplace helps you find and compare cloud technology and people to deliver digital projects.

It uses 3 frameworks (agreements between government and suppliers).

Cloud services (eg web hosting or IT health checks) are on the G-Cloud framework.

Specialists who can work on digital projects (eg technical architects and user researchers) are on the Digital Services framework.

Physical datacentre space for legacy systems is available on the Crown Hosting Data Centres framework.

All public sector organisations, including agencies and arm’s length bodies, can use the Digital Marketplace. Check the list of approved organisations to see if you’re eligible.

All Digital Marketplace suppliers have successfully been through the Official Journal of the European Union (OJEU) process. That means you can save on the time and cost traditionally associated with entering into individual procurement contracts.

2. Digital Marketplace services

There are over 19,000 cloud-based services on the Digital Marketplace, split into 4 categories:

  • Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS)
  • Platform as a Service (PaaS)
  • Software as a Service (SaaS)
  • Specialist Cloud Services (SCS)

More than 165 suppliers provide digital specialists to perform all or some of the following roles:

One supplier provides datacentre hosting to government. It offers:

  • a flexible, pay-for-what-you-use model
  • secure facilities
  • power uptime availability that exceeds Tier III+
  • leading environmental performance

You must still follow best practice for procurement when using the Digital Marketplace, which includes making your decision-making process auditable.

3. Preparing your requirements

We recommend working with both procurement and technical professionals to establish the requirements and timescales of your project.

You should consider:

  • the funds you have available to buy the service
  • the technical and procurement requirements of the project
  • the selection criteria on which your choice will be based

Identify what the ‘must-have’ requirements are and what the ‘wants’ are through an outcome-based request for proposal (RFP). Refer to the government's Service Design Manual to help you describe your needs.

GDS holds the government’s digital and IT spend control. This means that all funding requests for new or redesigned digital services need formal approval by GDS before they can go ahead. You’ll also need to have approval from within your organisation.

4. Buying Digital Marketplace services

The buying process is slightly different depending on whether you’re buying cloud-based services from the G-Cloud framework, digital specialist services from the Digital Services framework or physical datacentre space from the Crown Hosting Data Centres framework.

Read the G-Cloud buyers’ guide to learn more about finding cloud technology and support.

Read the Digital Services buyers’ guide to learn more about finding specialists to work on digital projects.

Read the Crown Hosting Data Centres framework guide to learn more about finding datacentre space for legacy systems.

If you have any questions relating to the Digital Marketplace or the buying process, please get in touch at enquiries@digitalmarketplace.service.gov.uk.
